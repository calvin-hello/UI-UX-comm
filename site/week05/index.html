    Week 05 Report

    Introduction

    This is the report of the event that took place in ACIT 2811 - UX/UI. The class focused on Information Architecture, exploring how content is structured and organized to improve user experience. We learned how clear organization, navigation, and labeling help users find information efficiently.

    Topics

    The main topics discussed in class were:

    1. Content Hierarchy
    2. Navigation Systems
    3. Labelling Systems
    4. Artifacts
    5. Sitemap

    Definition of Topics

    • Content Hierarchy refers to the organization and prioritization of information in a way that guides users through content logically and efficiently.
    • Navigation Systems are the structures that allow users to move through a product or website, helping them find information quickly and easily.
    • Labelling Systems involve the use of clear and consistent names for categories, menus, and content so users can easily understand what each section represents.
    • Artifacts are visual representations (such as diagrams, sketches, or wireframes) used to communicate design ideas and structure.
    • Sitemap is a diagram that shows how different pages and sections of a website are organized and connected.

    Content Hierarchy Principles

    Content hierarchy helps present information in a clear and meaningful order. It typically involves:

    1. Prioritizing important information – placing key content where users will notice it first.
    2. Organizing content logically – grouping related information together so users can easily understand how sections are connected.
    3. Guiding user attention – using headings, layout, and structure to naturally lead users through the content.

    Navigation Systems Structure

    Navigation systems are designed to help users move through content smoothly. They typically include:

    1. Clear pathways – allowing users to easily move between different sections.
    2. Consistency – keeping navigation elements uniform across pages to avoid confusion.
    3. Ease of use – ensuring users can quickly find what they are looking for with minimal effort.

    In-class Activities

    These are the in-class activities completed during the session.

    The first activity focused on organizing content hierarchy, where we structured information to improve clarity and usability. This helped us understand how important it is to present information based on user needs.

    The second activity involved creating a sitemap, where we mapped out the structure of a website and showed how different pages are connected. This emphasized the role of navigation and labeling in guiding users.

    We also created supporting artifacts such as diagrams and layouts to visually represent our ideas.
