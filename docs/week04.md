## Definition of Topics

* **Problem Statements** clearly define the issue being addressed, focusing on the user, their needs, and the context of the problem.
* **Ideation** is the process of generating a wide range of ideas and possible solutions, often through brainstorming techniques.
* **Artifacts** are tangible outputs (such as sketches, diagrams, or notes) that represent ideas and help communicate design thinking.
* **Feature Lists** outline the key functionalities or characteristics that a product or solution should include.

## Problem Statement Structure

A well-formed problem statement typically includes:

1. The target user
2. The user’s need or goal
3. The problem or pain point

## Ideation Process

1. Brainstorm ideas freely
2. Explore multiple solutions
3. Refine and select promising ideas
